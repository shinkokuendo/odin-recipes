Adobo Recipe

Filipino Pork Adobo with Pineapples

Filipino's adobo is known all through out the world. The taste varies depending on where you are from. Mostly, we don't put that much ingredients in it. Well, the reason is simple, we want to eat pork that is flavorful but we want it to be cheap. We filipinos were known to be a family oriented people and of course, whatever our meal is, should be shared with everyone in the family. You can just cook the pork with soy sauce, vinegar, and sugar, salt and pepper to taste. Though, sometimes, we replace sugar with pineapples. So, you see, assumming that you had a sugar, salt, pepper, soy and vinegar in your house, the only thing that you will buy is the pork. That is how practical that dish is.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg of pork
  • 1/2 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup of vinegar
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 pouch of pineapple
  • half head of garlic
  • 4-5 bay leaves
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a little bit of cooking oil
  1. Heat up your cooking pot. Once heated, pour a little bit of oil in it.
  2. Then, put the garlic. Wait it until brown.
  3. Add the pork. Fry it until half cooked.
  4. Add a little bit of salt to add some flavor in the pork. Make sure that it is not too much.
  5. Add the soy sauce, vinegar and the water.
  6. When it is almost done, add the pineapple and the bay leaves.
  7. And lastly, the salt and pepper depending on your taste.
